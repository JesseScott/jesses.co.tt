"Jesse Scott creates and exhibits work in the genre formerly known as new media."


He is:
an artist + creative coder,
designer + visualist,
curator + producer,
writer + educator.

Under various aliases and operating within several artist collectives, he produces work for live performance, for site-specific actions, for listening, for viewing, for readingâ€¦

His work has spanned the domains of a/v performance, locative media, telematics, improvisational practice, urban projection, installation design, smartphone applications, public workshops, and the written word.

Alongside Mirae Rosner, he ran the memelab, and co-founded both the Canadian and German chapters of Graffiti Research Lab, and the Vancouver and Berlin chapters of Processing Cities.

Formerly, he was a member of The Butchershop, a non-denominational art space, NTSC, an audio-visual collective, and was President of Balcone Art Society.

Jesse has curated for the memelab, New Forms Festival, Balcone Art Society, Mobile Muse, The Butchershop, Nuclei of Telematic Sound and Cinematics, The Upgrade!, SWARM, and Gallery Gachet.

He currently teaches creative programming and smartphone development at several universities and media arts centres in Vancouver, and has led workshops across North America and Europe.

He has been the recipient of several awards, grants, artist residencies, and commissions, and has been exhibited extensively throughout North America, Europe, and Asia.
